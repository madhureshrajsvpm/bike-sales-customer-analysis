# 🚲 Bike Sales Customer Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes customer demographic, socioeconomic, and behavioural data to identify key factors influencing bike purchases.

The goal is to derive actionable insights for improving marketing strategies, customer targeting, and product positioning.

---

## 🛠️ Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel
- Pivot Tables
- Data Cleaning Techniques
- Data Visualization (Charts, Dashboard)

---

## 🔍 Key Steps Performed

### 1. Data Cleaning
- Removed duplicates and handled missing values
- Standardized categorical variables (Gender, Marital Status)
- Created new features:
  - Age Groups
  - Above Average Income
  - Car Ownership

### 2. Exploratory Data Analysis
- Analyzed purchase patterns across:
  - Age groups
  - Income levels
  - Commute distance
  - Region
  - Occupation
  - Education

---

## 📊 Key Insights

- Only **48.1% customers are buyers**, indicating **51.9% untapped market**
- **Middle-aged individuals** are the highest buyers (~52%)
- Customers commuting **2–5 miles** are most likely to purchase bikes
- **Female customers have slightly higher conversion rates than males**
- **Pacific region** shows highest purchase rate (~59%)
- Customers with **1 child or none** are more likely to buy bikes
- **Professionals and clerical workers** show higher buying behavior

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

- Target **middle-aged professionals** with focused campaigns
- Increase marketing in high-performing regions (Pacific)
- Design **women-focused product strategies**
- Promote bikes as **lifestyle/recreational products**
- Introduce **family-friendly bike solutions**
- Offer financing options for non-homeowners

---

## 📈 Conclusion
Bike purchase decisions are driven more by **lifestyle and commute patterns** rather than income alone. Strategic segmentation can significantly improve conversion rates.
